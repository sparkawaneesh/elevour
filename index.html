FAQ - Frequently Asked Questions

In this section, you can find more information about the product if you still have questions.

A Unified Health Interface (UHI) is a type of software interface that is designed to facilitate the exchange of healthcare information between different systems and stakeholders. It provides a standardized way for different healthcare systems, such as electronic health record (EHR) systems, to communicate and share data with each other. The goal of a UHI is to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of healthcare delivery by enabling healthcare providers to access and share relevant patient information more easily. This can help to reduce errors and improve patient care. Some common features of a UHI include the ability to access and view patient records, order and track lab tests, and manage patient care plans. UHIs may be used in a variety of settings, including hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilities.
